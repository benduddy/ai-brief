Monday Reopening Brief

6 July 2026 · 48 names · benchmark QQQ · data 07:31 UTC

Wall Street reopens today after the long Independence Day weekend — and it reopens into a cooler, more hostile tape. The snapshot behind this brief was taken before the US open, so every American stock in it is still sitting on Thursday's close (Friday was shut) — the third session running with frozen equity prices. What actually moved overnight tells the story: the model's only two changes are both cooling flips — Siemens Energy and Solana each dropped out of "Leading" — while Friday's weak jobs report and a hawkish Fed leave the AI leaders digesting, not breaking out.

  • How to read the scores: heat 0 = washed-out / cheap-looking, 100 = red-hot / overextended; momentum = strength of the recent up-move; status bands are Leading (strong & healthy), Overheated (strong but stretched), Basing (weak / bottoming), Neutral (no clear signal). Forward PEG = forward P/E divided by expected earnings growth — under ~1 you're paying little for a lot of growth, over ~2 the opposite. The Setup lines are opinions on risk/reward and timing to weigh — not directives; the call is yours.
  • US equities are still frozen at Thursday's close. The overnight data refresh is genuinely newer, but with markets shut Friday and not yet open this morning, the American stock prices it captured haven't changed since Thursday 2 July. So the stock levels below are the same as the last two briefs — leaned on lightly and referenced, not re-pasted. The live action is in Europe (Siemens Energy) and crypto.
  • Both overnight moves are cooling flips. Only two names changed status, and both went the same direction — down a notch: Siemens Energy Leading → Neutral (a ~1.7% Xetra pullback that landed it right back on its 50-day) and Solana Leading → Neutral (momentum easing). No name flipped up. The froth stays absent: still zero Overheated names, and the hottest score on the whole board is unchanged at Astera Labs, heat 57.
  • The macro turned less friendly. Friday's jobs report was weak — only 57,000 jobs vs ~114k expected — which actually trimmed Fed rate-hike odds (this Fed, under Chair Warsh, is debating a hike, not a cut). Semis sold off a second day into the holiday (the chip ETF SMH ~-4.5%, Micron ~-5.5%, Nvidia ~-1.4%) while the Dow hit record highs — classic money rotating out of the crowded AI-semi trade into the broad market. The model's cooling read and this rotation are the same story.
  • Where value still sits (unchanged): the leaders that are both near an entry and cheap for their growth — AMD (PEG 0.5), Siemens Energy (0.73), GE Vernova (0.75). The pricier A-grade names, where the move is the trend not the valuation, remain Alphabet (PEG ~10.5), NextEra (2.23) and Astera Labs (2.19).
  • Weak tape, strong future — accumulation candidates: the mirror image of the above — names quiet or below trend now but cheap for strong forward growth. Cleanest AI-compute example is Nvidia (Basing, but PEG ~0.36 on ~42% growth — see its feature), with Broadcom (PEG ~0.28) the next one down. Low momentum here is being early, not late.
  • The calendar is still the catalyst. Nothing forces the frozen stocks' hand until they trade today, then it stays quiet until the heavy day: 22 July brings GE Vernova and Alphabet earnings plus AMD's "Advancing AI" event, with the Fed (Chair Warsh) on 28–29 July — now a hike-vs-hold debate — the marquee risk for both chips and crypto.
