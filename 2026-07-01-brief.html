Wednesday Signal Brief

1 July 2026 · 48 names · benchmark QQQ · data 08:08 UTC

The rally didn't just hold — it got hot. Tuesday's US session pushed the Dow and Nasdaq to fresh records and the semis index to a record close, and the model swallowed that tape whole: thirteen names changed status and seven of them tipped from merely-strong into stretched. A week ago the worry was the sell-off; today it's that the leaders are running far above their own recent baselines with less and less cushion beneath them.

  • How to read the scores: heat 0 = washed-out / cheap-looking, 100 = red-hot / overextended; momentum = strength of the recent up-move; status bands are Leading (strong & healthy), Overheated (strong but stretched), Basing (weak / bottoming), Neutral (no clear signal). The Setup lines are opinions on risk/reward and timing to weigh — not directives; the call is yours.

  • The one still sitting it out: Nvidia — still Basing, still under the ~$196 line a source flagged, still the worst-performing big semi this year while everything around it makes records. The laggard story is now three weeks old.

Indices

Tuesday 30 Jun was a second straight record day: the Dow closed at a record ~52,319, the Nasdaq 100 rose ~1.7%, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index made a record close. The AI-infrastructure trade is doing the heavy lifting — money is paying up specifically for the companies building out compute, power and cooling.

Macro

The Fed held rates and struck a hawkish tone last week; there was one day of selling and then the buy side took it all back. The read: institutions want the AI builders badly enough to pay record prices even into a Fed trying to cool things down. The flip side — almost everything outside semis is feeling higher rates and energy costs, so the AI trade is carrying the index largely on its own.

Smart money (from the wiki)

Jordi Visser (28 Jun) framed June's hyperscaler drawdown as a rotation, not a top — long the thematic AI basket, breadth at highs, 200-day rock solid. Tuesday's broad melt-up into semis and power (not just defensives) keeps cutting his way. His structural centrepiece stays memory: “there is nowhere near enough” — no line of sight to supply catching demand — which is why deep-value MU/SNDK keep screening cheap. InvestAnswers still holds Nvidia as core “steady-Eddie” ballast (~20× forward, ~$196 support), even as it lags. Where they'd diverge from today's tape: both prefer buying weakness, and seven names just went the other way — stretched.
