Thursday Signal Brief

2 July 2026 · 48 names · benchmark QQQ · data 07:29 UTC

The pullback the model kept flagging arrived. Wednesday — the first session of Q3 — the AI-chip complex sold off hard: Corning −14%, Micron and Sandisk −10.6%, Intel −9%, AMD −6.9%. That single-day cool-off drained the froth: every name that tipped into overheated on Tuesday reset back to merely-Leading — eleven of them in one go. Nothing broke; the leaders just came in from the highs and, in several cases, back toward a price you can actually buy.

  • How to read the scores: heat 0 = washed-out / cheap-looking, 100 = red-hot / overextended; momentum = strength of the recent up-move; status bands are Leading (strong & healthy), Overheated (strong but stretched), Basing (weak / bottoming), Neutral (no clear signal). Forward PEG = forward P/E divided by expected earnings growth — under ~1 you're paying little for a lot of growth, over ~2 the opposite. The Setup lines are opinions on risk/reward and timing to weigh — not directives; the call is yours.
  • Hot cohort cooled in a day. Wednesday's chip-led pullback (Nasdaq −0.66%, semis index the worst of it) took the whole overheated group down 7–14%. All eleven that were stretched on Tuesday — TSM, ASML, AMD, MRVL, INTC, ALAB, MU, SNDK, GLW, ENTG, GEV — flipped back to Leading. This is the model working as intended: heat came out, trend stayed intact.
  • What the cool-off created: a cluster of leaders that are now near an entry and cheap for their growth — the pullback-hunter's bullseye. MRVL (PEG 0.8), AMD (0.5), TSM (0.8), GEV (0.8), BE (0.6), plus deep-value memory MU / SNDK (~0.1). Leading-but-pricey of the group: Intel (PEG ~1.9) and ALAB (~2.3) — the move there is the story, not the valuation.
  • Two that went the other way: Vertiv — yesterday's clean new entry — flipped Leading → Basing on a −7% day and slipped below its 50-day. Fluence did the same. Momentum, not thesis, cracked there.
  • Still sitting it out: Nvidia — still Basing, still the cheapest big semi (PEG 0.4), still hovering just above the ~$196 line a source flagged while the suppliers ran. Bitcoin at ~$60K is washed-out (heat 5) near its much-watched triple-bottom.

Market backdrop

first day of Q3 — profit-taking out of the hottest names
TAPE · The session behind the numbers

Tape

Wednesday 1 Jul, the first session of Q3: Nasdaq −0.66%, S&P −0.22%, Dow roughly flat — but the damage was concentrated in chips and memory. Micron −10.6%, Sandisk −10.6%, Applied Materials −10%, Intel −9%, AMD −6.9%, Corning −14%. After a monster Q2 (Nasdaq +20%, S&P +14%), money took profits out of the names that had run the most. This is rotation and profit-taking, not a fundamental break — the exact 'digest the melt-up' move the model was set up for.

Macro

Jobs data in focus ahead of Friday's payrolls: ADP showed soft private hiring (+98k, a touch light), JOLTS openings held at ~7.6M. Fed Chair Warsh said inflation expectations have eased over the past month — the hawkish scare from a few weeks ago has largely unwound. Rate-cut odds firming is a tailwind for the rate-sensitive, non-mega-cap parts of the market.

What the smart money is saying (from the wiki)

Jordi Visser (28 Jun, newest): this is consolidation inside a bull market, not a top — 'the market's way of making a winning trend harder to own.' His thematic basket's rate-of-change is slowing (growth decelerating, not reversing); the 200-day trend is 'rock solid,' breadth near highs. Stance unchanged: long the thematic AI basket, and he's most vocal on memory being genuinely supply-constrained ('no line of sight' to when supply catches demand). InvestAnswers (mid-June) has been the buy-the-dip voice — treats washouts like this as accumulation, not exit. They don't really diverge here: both read a sharp cool-off in a still-intact uptrend. See [[ai-midcycle-slowdown-jordi-visser]], [[ai-trade-rotation]].
