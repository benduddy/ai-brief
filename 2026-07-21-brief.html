GEV · GE Vernova$1,079
LeadingEntry A · prime addStrong & cheapPower & Grid

Bullish

The strongest, healthiest name in the book today — momentum 83, heat 41, and the only grade-A name that's also cheap for its growth. Gas turbines and grid gear are the “layer under the layer” of the AI buildout, and demand is booked out for years: Q1 backlog ~$163B, data-center electrification orders in one quarter beat all of last year, and management pulled its $200B-backlog target forward a year.

Bearish

Turbine build slots are effectively sold through ~2030, so backlog is visibility, not an automatic revenue multiplier — near-term revenue is capped by how fast it can build. It carries the richest multiple in the power group, so a lot of the good news is already in the price.

Valuation

Forward P/E ~44 with next-year earnings expected to grow ~62%, which gives a forward PEG of ~0.71 — that's price/earnings divided by expected growth; under ~1 means you're paying little for a lot of growth, over ~2 means the opposite. So despite the high headline P/E, GEV screens as reasonably priced for how fast it's growing — Visser's exact sweet spot (strong trend, low PEG).

Forward view

If the $163B→$200B backlog converts, that points to a materially higher revenue run-rate by 2027–28 versus this year. The catch is the same as the bull case: conversion is gated by physical build capacity, so the forward story is about steady, visible growth rather than a step-change the market hasn't seen. At today's multiple, much of that is arguably priced — you're buying quality and visibility, not a cheap re-rating.

Levels

Trading ~4% above its 50-day (~$1,035) and ~8% below its 52-week high (~$1,175) — i.e. near its highs but with a real trend cushion under it. The 50-day is the line to watch: holding above it keeps this a clean uptrend.
