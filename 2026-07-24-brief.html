Market Backdrop

Neutral24 Jul 2026

Tape

Read it as two things at different levels — oil loaded the gun, earnings pulled the trigger. The backdrop is a jittery, risk-off tape: Brent is elevated near ~$85 with US strikes on Tehran and the Hormuz naval blockade reinstated this week — the cross-portfolio oil-shock tail that raises the market's risk premium and leaves it no patience to forgive a miss. Against that nervous backdrop, the trigger was earnings: Alphabet (−7%) and Tesla (−14.5%) fell on the size of their AI spend and the hit to free cash flow, dragging Amazon −4.6% in sympathy ahead of its own print. Nasdaq-100 −2%, S&P −1.2% on 23 Jul; the semis ETF (SMH) has given back most of its Q2 gain.

Not a pure oil-shock (the tell)

The cross-section says this wasn't a blanket macro flush. A true Hormuz risk-off is broad-beta — high-multiple names down, energy/commodities up. Today the opposite: the highest-beta AI names were green (MU +3.2%, GEV +4.7%, CIFR +5.6%) while the classic oil-shock beneficiaries in the book were red (MP −2.3%, UUUU −2.4% rare-earth/uranium, solar all lower), and NVDA — the most oil-exposed mega-cap — only fell −1.6%. Oil raised the risk premium and set the mood; the actual damage was company-specific (Tesla's margin/cash-flow miss, Alphabet's capex guide). And money rotated within the AI trade — out of the spenders, into the receivers — rather than fleeing it, which says the AI-trade demand isn't what broke.

The valuation read

This is being called a mid-cycle reset on valuation, not a demand collapse: the underlying numbers (Google Cloud +82%, Dell AI orders +88%) are still strong. The nuance the tape drew — and the reason the receivers held — is that investors now want the capex to visibly earn, so they'd rather own the names that get paid as the buildout happens than the ones still spending toward a payoff.

Rates

The Fed decides on 29 July and is widely expected to hold at 3.50–3.75% (~90% odds). June inflation cooled (CPI ~3.5% annual, softer than expected), so the near-term risk driving the tape has shifted from rates to capex returns. No obvious macro landmine before the meeting; the next hard test is Amazon's Q2 on 30 Jul.

Smart money (from the wiki)

Today is the wiki's central thesis playing out. Jordi Visser has argued for months to underweight the hyperscalers and overweight what they spend on — power, memory, optical, hardware ("your capex is my opportunity"); the tape drew exactly that split. He's also framed recent weakness as a "speed-crash / leverage flush," not a top, with the memory shortage running "beyond 2030." InvestAnswers (8 Jul): summer is for sniping dips — NVDA's $190–195 shelf is the deeper support (price ~$209), AVGO's ~$360 line the reversal level, and he'd still avoid Marvell ("broke down"), which the tape still agrees with (MRVL ~22% below its 50-day).

The fresh test

This is the mirror of yesterday. Yesterday the power flagship GEV missed and fell −8.6%, testing the wiki's "priced for its backlog" caveat. Today it bounced +4.7% and flipped to Leading — the market looked past the profit-quality wobble to the raised guidance and ~$176B backlog. Meanwhile the hyperscalers that fund the whole buildout are the ones being sold. The through-line: capex isn't stopping — Google just raised it — but investors now want to see it earn, and until they do they'd rather own the suppliers than the spenders.
