Friday Signal Brief

3 July 2026 · 48 names · benchmark QQQ · data 07:24 UTC

The cool-off became a rout. A second straight down-day hit the AI-chip complex hard — SanDisk −14%, Cipher −12%, Entegris and Corning ~−11%, Marvell −10%, Tesla −7.5%. Almost everything red; only the power names, a couple of coins and Siemens Energy finished up. Yesterday the froth drained and the leaders stayed Leading. Today the selling pushed several of them a rung lower — seven names lost their Leading or Neutral status and slipped into Basing. Trends are bending now, not just cooling.

  • How to read the scores: heat 0 = washed-out / cheap-looking, 100 = red-hot / overextended; momentum = strength of the recent up-move; status bands are Leading (strong & healthy), Overheated (strong but stretched), Basing (weak / bottoming), Neutral (no clear signal). Forward PEG = forward P/E divided by expected earnings growth — under ~1 you're paying little for a lot of growth, over ~2 the opposite. The Setup lines are opinions on risk/reward and timing to weigh — not directives; the call is yours.
  • Day two of the chip rout. The VanEck semis ETF (SMH) has now given back a big slice of its record +71% Q2. The trigger cluster: a report that SK Hynix is slowing its HBM (high-bandwidth-memory) expansion, a Meta plan to resell spare data-centre capacity (the phrase "excess capacity" spooks an AI trade built on ever-rising capex), plus lingering valuation nerves. Memory took the worst of it.
  • The froth is fully gone — arguably overshot. Zero names are Overheated now (Dell, yesterday's last one, cooled to Leading). No name has a heat score above 57. This is no longer "stretched leaders"; several are now genuinely oversold on a two-day drop.
  • Where the drop created value: a cluster of leaders is now near an entry and cheap for its growth — AMD (PEG 0.5), Siemens Energy (0.74), GE Vernova (0.75), plus deep-value memory SanDisk / Micron (~0.05). Leading-but-pricey exceptions: Alphabet (PEG ~10, the trend not the valuation) and Astera Labs (~2.2).
  • Two source levels got tested today: Marvell fell into IA's ~$240–255 buy zone (now $245), and Nvidia broke below the ~$196 line a source said had "held on the nose" — now $194.83, the first close under that base. Watch whether it reclaims it.
  • The standout: Siemens Energy bucked the whole tape, +6.6% and upgraded Basing → Leading — the electrification/power leg doing exactly the job Jordi Visser flagged it for: an AI bottleneck that also hedges the chip names when they wobble.
