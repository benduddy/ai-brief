Market Backdrop

the tape + what the smart money is saying

The tape

Friday was the mirror image of Thursday's broad melt-up: instead of everything rising together, leadership narrowed to the highest-quality names while the speculative edge sold off. The chip index (SOX) closed back above its average price of the last 50 days after dipping below it earlier in the week, but it's still ~11% under its June peak — a repair, not a fresh breakout. Chips actually wobbled early Friday, then NVDA led the tape after headlines that the US will relax AI-chip export limits for the UAE — a new overseas demand lane for Nvidia, AMD and Cerebras. Goldman called Nvidia's ~22x forward multiple "compelling." Underneath, the froth came out: Bitcoin miners gave back Thursday's bounce and a few hot small-caps (WOLF −5.3%) fell hard.

Smart money (from the wiki)

Two lenses, both structurally bullish, split on timing — and today's action tested both. InvestAnswers (8-Jul) said NVDA's ~$190–195 support had held since April and he expected it to work back toward ~$228; Friday's breakout to $211 is that call starting to play out — so this is the thesis confirming, not a fresh re-paste (see recent briefs for the full setup). His stance stays "snipe the dips in the leaders, don't chase." Jordi Visser (5-Jul) reads this as mid-cycle digestion, not a top, and favours the suppliers over the hyperscaler spenders ("your capex is my opportunity"), rotating toward the application layer plus power and optical. Where today pokes at Jordi: Amazon repairing (Lagging→Basing) is a spender turning up, a mild counter to the rotation-away-from-hyperscalers idea. Net: a healthier, more selective tape that leans IA's way — the leader led, the froth cooled.
