Market Backdrop

the tape + what the smart money is saying

The tape

A broad, two-handed sell-off. The AI-faith hand: China's Moonshot AI released Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open model that anyone can download free from 27 Jul — reviving the “DeepSeek-moment” worry that cheaper models erode demand for expensive compute; on top of that TSMC's strong revenue but heavy capex guide stoked AI-cost-sustainability fears, and Alphabet's Gemini 3.5 Pro slipped its launch. Global semis have shed ~$3.3 trillion since 22 Jun and the chip ETF is off ~17% on the month. The macro risk-off hand: a sixth day of US airstrikes on Iran with the Strait of Hormuz effectively shut pushed oil up and pulled money out of risk assets — which is why crypto eased even as it out-performed the semis. Net: the correction broadened from the hot names to the whole complex. The demand read-through, though, is still fine — TSMC's Q2 beat and the multi-year memory contracts haven't changed. This reads as a valuation-and-positioning reset amplified by a geopolitical scare, not a demand break. Macro focus now shifts to the FOMC on 28–29 Jul (market still prices a hold).

Smart money (from the wiki)

Jordi Visser's washout call is exactly this tape. His 12-Jul thesis (referenced, not re-pasted) framed the AI mid-cycle slowdown as ending, not deepening — a leverage flush inside an ongoing bull, with ~87% of semis oversold as a historically bottoming signal, and the memory shortage now stretching “beyond 2030” — see [[ai-midcycle-slowdown-jordi-visser]] and [[memory-chips]]. Today's broad flush, on a China-model headline plus an oil/Iran risk-off rather than a demand miss, is the kind of setup that thesis says to accumulate into. InvestAnswers (8-Jul) has been consistent all summer: “snipe the dips in leaders, don't chase,” long-term bullish on memory (MU ~$1,600) and on AVGO (buy on the Apple deal, WS target ~$520). The disagreement to note: the signal model now has the leaders Basing/below-trend, while both followed sources read the pullback as opportunity — the split is about timing (wait for a base vs accumulate now), not direction. And Jordi's new angle — Kimi K3 is demand for inference tokens, not a threat to it — is the bull rebuttal to today's China-model scare.
