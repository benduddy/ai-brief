Thursday Signal Brief

25 June 2026 · 48 names · benchmark QQQ · data 08:06 UTC

Yesterday was calm; today the board actually moved — and it moved down. A broad cool-down hit the miners, the rare-earth/materials names and even Nvidia (which slipped into Basing), while a handful of power-and-fibre names bucked the tape higher. The marquee event resolved overnight: Micron's earnings were a blowout — yet the stock barely moved.

  • A risk-off cool-down, not a calm day. Eight status changes (vs one yesterday): NVDA slipped Neutral→Basing, IREN and Steel Dynamics dropped out of Leading, USA Rare Earth and Wolfspeed fell into Basing. Momentum drained from the crypto miners (Cipher, MARA, CleanSpark all cooled hard) and the rare-earth complex. The hot semis themselves barely budged.
  • Micron's print was a blowout — but the stock didn't react. Revenue $41.5B (vs ~$35.6B expected), EPS $25.11 (vs ~$20.60), and a stunning Q4 guide of $50B (vs ~$42.9B). HBM4 already past $1B revenue, ramping ~2× as fast as the prior generation, and management says it has no line of sight to when supply catches demand. Yet the model shows MU essentially flat day-over-day — a muted reaction that itself is the story (see below).
  • Two names bucked the cool-down: Corning (GLW) jumped ~+6% with heat +20 on its Amazon data-centre fibre deal, and Bloom Energy (BE) flipped into Overheated on a pile-up of AI-power deals. Power and fibre are where today's bid went.
  • What the smart money is saying (wiki): today's Micron blowout lands squarely on the IA-vs-Jordi fork. InvestAnswers (structurally bullish, 'demand for memory is infinite', long-term ~$3,000) looks vindicated by the guide; Jordi Visser (exited MU in the $600–700 area, 'top likely in', 'you can't linearly extrapolate token growth into memory demand') is challenged by it — but the flat price reaction is exactly the 'it's already priced in' tell he'd point to. Both views still live.
  • Still the cheapest-for-growth: MU, AMD, NVDA, MRVL, CIFR, SNDK, TSM and GEV all screen well — leaders that haven't run their valuations up with their prices (Visser's pullback-hunter sweet spot). One to flag the other way: ARM is leading but pricey (PEG ~2.8) — its move is in the trend, not the valuation.

Memory is cyclical, and a low P/E on peak-cycle earnings can flatter the picture. SK Hynix has reportedly been slowing advanced-AI-chip output — a hint the tightest part of the cycle could be loosening even as Micron says the opposite.

Valuation

Forward P/E ~8, next-year growth ~+94%, forward PEG ~0.09 — optically the cheapest name in the whole universe. But read it the way you'd read any cyclical: a rock-bottom P/E on top-of-cycle earnings is exactly what a peak can look like. Cheap on the screen — the question the flat reaction is asking is whether the market believes those earnings hold.
