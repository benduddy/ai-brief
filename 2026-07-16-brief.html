The whole overheated complex cooled at once. Forward PEG = price/earnings divided by expected growth — under ~1 means you're paying little for a lot of growth, over ~2 the opposite. Market Backdrop

the tape + what the smart money is saying

The tape

TSMC's Q2 beat today is the session's positive — ~$40.2B revenue, gross margin ~68%, Q3 guided up to ~$44.6–45.8B, and management called AI demand “extremely robust.” The demand read-through is good. But the AI-hardware and memory complex de-rated on three overlapping worries: over-build (a leaked Meta plan to lease out surplus AI capacity hit Dell and the other server builders), margin squeeze (rising DRAM/HBM costs eat into thin server margins), and China competition (memory-maker CXMT's $8.55B IPO threatens long-run DRAM pricing power). Net effect: money rotated out of the capex-heavy box-makers and commodity memory, toward the fabless designers (NVDA, AMD, AVGO) and power/grid. Macro is quiet into the FOMC on 28–29 Jul (market prices ~80% no change); June CPI ran 3.5% year-on-year.

Smart money (from the wiki)

Jordi Visser's memory call is being live-tested today. His 12-Jul thesis (used as yesterday's pull-quote, so not repeated in full) held that the memory shortage now stretches “beyond 2030” and that Samsung selling off on a 19-fold profit beat was a technical unwind, not a demand failure — see [[memory-chips]]. Today's ~8% flush in MU and SNDK, on a China-competition headline rather than a demand miss, is exactly the setup that thesis says to accumulate into, not fear. He exited Micron in mid-May and has been eyeing a re-entry after the SK Hynix deal. InvestAnswers (8-Jul) still carries a long-term MU target of ~$1,600 while flagging near-term mean-reversion lower — which is playing out — and rates AVGO a buy on the Apple deal (WS avg target ~$520). The disagreement to note: the signal model has memory Basing/flushing, while the followed sources still see the shortage as structural — the split is about timing, not direction.
