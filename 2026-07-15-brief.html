Market Backdrop

the tape + what the smart money is saying

The tape

A real risk-off session in chips, but a selective one. The most-extended, richest-valued semis were sold hardest — ARM −13%, ALAB −12%, SNDK −8%, QCOM −6%, MRVL −6% — while the mega-cap anchors held (NVDA +0.4%) and some names rose outright (DELL +5%). This is the shape of a positioning/valuation shakeout in the froth, not a broad demand scare. Helpfully, the fundamental news this week has been good: yesterday's June inflation print came in soft (headline cooling toward ~3.5% YoY), easing rate pressure, and ASML reported today and raised its 2026 sales guidance — a bright spot, not a warning. The chip index (SOX) had actually bounced ~2.5% into yesterday before today's high-beta names rolled over. The next real read is TSM's Q2 tomorrow.

Smart money (from the wiki)

The timely voice is Jordi Visser (12 Jul), whose latest read is that the mid-cycle slowdown is ending, not deepening — a technical washout (a leverage flush), not a market top. He pointed to ~87% of semis being oversold as a historically bottoming signal, and a fresh MACD buy on NVDA at a decade-low valuation. Today's further leg down in the hottest chips is the live test of that call — the drops being insider-sale / rotation driven (not demand) fits his "washout" framing. InvestAnswers (8 Jul, referenced — see prior briefs) has stayed on the same page all summer: "snipe the dips in the leaders, don't chase," with a live NVDA ~$205 support / $228 target. His Astera re-entry zone ($379–381 for a trade back toward $500) is now undercut by today's $362 — noted below. Jordi's structural tilt: long scarcity (memory, power, people), short abundance — which is exactly where today's tape rotated (power/grid up, frothy compute down).
