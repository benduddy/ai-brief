Tuesday Pre-Open Brief — the AI trade roars back

30 June 2026 · 48 names · benchmark QQQ · data Mon 29 Jun close · synced Tue 08:09 UTC

The mirror image of yesterday. Monday's US session — the first real tape since Friday's sell-off — didn't just steady, it ripped: the Nasdaq 100 jumped ~2.3% to a record high as money poured back into the names it had been fleeing a week earlier. The model just swallowed that session, and it shows: nine names changed status, three of them tipped from merely-strong into stretched, and the optical and semis leaders printed double-digit single-day gains. After a frozen weekend, the AI trade came back loud.

  • How to read the scores: heat 0 = washed-out / cheap-looking, 100 = red-hot / overextended; momentum = strength of the recent up-move; status bands are Leading (strong & healthy), Overheated (strong but stretched), Basing (weak / bottoming), Neutral (no clear signal). The Setup lines are opinions on risk/reward and timing to weigh — not directives; the call is yours.
  • Risk-on, broad. Friday's worst-week-since-March reversed hard on Monday: Nasdaq 100 +2.3% to a record, semis ETF +~3%, Tesla +8.5%, Amazon +5.3%, helped by easing US–Iran tension. The model's day-over-day diff (Fri close → Mon close) is the cleanest snapshot of that bounce.
  • What flipped: three leaders ran hot — ASML, AMD, Astera Labs all tipped Leading → Overheated. Two woke up — Eaton and Bloom Energy flipped Neutral → Leading. A few faded — Steel Dynamics, Enphase, First Solar, Applied Opto all cooled a band.
  • Strong & still cheap-ish (the pullback-hunter bullseye): leaders that are buyable on timing and reasonably priced for their growth — MRVL (PEG 0.9), AMD (0.5), TSM (0.8), GEV (0.7), ETN (1.5), plus deep-value memory MU / SNDK (~0.1). The hot optical pair (GLW, ALAB) are leading but no longer cheap.
  • The one that sat it out: Nvidia — still Basing, still under the ~$196 line a source flagged, while almost everything else rallied. The laggard story continues.

What happened

Monday 29 Jun was a sharp relief rally — the AI trade's first session after the worst week since March, and it bounced hard. S&P 500 +1.2%, Nasdaq 100 +2.3% to a fresh record high, the Dow to a record; the VanEck semis ETF up ~3%. Mega-caps led: Tesla +8.5%, Amazon +5.3%, Alphabet +3.9% (its first day as a Dow member, replacing Verizon). Helping the mood: easing US–Iran tension over the weekend, with talks set to resume, plus an $8B Rocket Lab–Iridium deal. This is the session our snapshot just captured.

Smart money (wiki)

Monday's tape is a near-real-time test of Jordi Visser's standing call (28 Jun, "Hyperscalers Are Collapsing"): that last week's hyperscaler drawdown was rotation inside a bull market, not a top — breadth and the 200-day intact, the hawkish-Fed scare unwound. The very next session the basket roared back to record highs, which is exactly what "rotation, not a top" predicts — one data point, but it cuts his way. InvestAnswers stays structurally bullish on memory and constructive on a crypto bottom (the ~$59K BTC "triple-bottom"). Where they'd diverge now: Jordi's tilt is toward the application layer from here (the big hardware multibaggers are "built in"); IA still trades the hardware leaders on levels. (Full theses in the 28–29 Jun briefs — not re-pasted here.)

GLW · Corning$255.69
OverheatedEntry C · waitheat 93Materials & Optical

Bullish

The day's hottest name and one of its biggest movers (+~16% in the session), printing a fresh record high. Corning has quietly become a core AI-optical supplier: optical sales grew ~36% last quarter, and the three biggest tech spenders have signed long-term fiber deals — an Amazon multi-year, multibillion-dollar optical-fiber contract (announced 8 Jun, ~1,000 new NC jobs), plus Nvidia tapping Corning to expand US optical-connectivity capacity ~10x, and Meta in the mix. That turns it from a one-customer AI story into a hyperscaler pattern.

Bearish

It's running near-vertical. The model reads it as parabolic — momentum pinned at 100, heat 93 (red-hot) — and it's sitting right on its 52-week high with no pullback to lean on. Single-session +16% moves into a record are exactly the conditions that mean-revert; the buying has been described as unusually heavy call-option speculation, which can reverse fast.

Valuation

Forward P/E ~60 on ~33% next-year earnings growth → forward PEG ~1.8. PEG is price/earnings divided by expected growth — under ~1 means you're paying little for a lot of growth, over ~2 the opposite. At ~1.8, Corning is fairly-to-fully priced for its growth: the optical story is real, but you're no longer buying it cheap.

Levels

Trading ~38% above its average price of the last 50 days (~$185) — stretched far above its recent baseline, with the 50-day the first real support a long way down. No overhead resistance (it's at the highs), so the risk here is air underneath, not a ceiling above.

Setup

Entry grade C — right stock, wrong price right now. Best-in-class optical demand, but ~38% above the 50-day into a parabolic record is a chase, not an entry. Model read: the risk/reward favours waiting for a pullback toward the rising 50-day, or at least a multi-day cool-off, rather than buying the green candle. Nothing here says the trend is broken — only that today's price is the worst part of an otherwise strong setup.
