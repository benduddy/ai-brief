Wednesday's rebound

The tape

Wednesday (8 Jul) was a relief bounce after Tuesday's semis-led wipeout. The trigger for Tuesday — a Reuters report that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI-inference chip — faded as analysts noted the read-through is marginal: Nvidia's China datacenter revenue is already near-zero under export limits. Networking, datacenter and memory names led the snap-back; the broad AI-hardware complex closed green. Capital that rotated into healthcare/financials on Tuesday partly rotated back. Still a two-sided tape, not an all-clear — the next hard macro read is June CPI on 14 Jul, then the Fed on 29 Jul.

Where they diverge: IA buys the semis dip; Jordi rotates toward power + optical + application. Today's rebound in the exact names both like (power, optical, big-cap semis) doesn't settle it — a bounce is not a trend.
