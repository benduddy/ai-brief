The tape

Thursday's session was a broad AI/semis melt-up — Nasdaq ~+0.9%, S&P ~+0.8%, and the chip complex leading (SOX/SMH up sharply). Micron rose ~7.5% on a reported $3B US-capex plan and dragged the whole memory group higher; optical (LITE +11%, AAOI +6.8%) and power/storage names joined in. Smart money (from the wiki)

Two lenses, both structurally bullish, split on timing. InvestAnswers (8-Jul): summer chop is "when we snipe" — buy dips in the leaders (NVDA ~$190–195, Micron on weakness), don't chase highs; runs 80% AI / 20% crypto. Jordi Visser (5-Jul): still mid-cycle digestion, not a top — "momentum crashing" is a crowded-trade washout, not danger; he favours the suppliers over the hyperscaler spenders ("your capex is my opportunity") and is rotating toward the application layer plus power/optical. Where they diverge: IA treats dips as gifts and targets memory much higher; Jordi is more patient and braces for a memory-supply peak around 2027. Thursday's broad rip in names both like doesn't settle it — a bounce isn't a trend.
