Tape

The AI trade is pausing for breath after roughly a five-week pullback — the Nasdaq-100 is down on the week and the semis (SOX) are soft on Asia-earnings jitters. The setup is oversold, so a bounce is on the table, but the signal book is doing something more constructive under the surface: leadership is broadening (NVDA rejoins, networking and power gear firm up) rather than one name going parabolic. Still zero overheated.

Rates

The Fed decides on 29 July and is widely expected to hold. June inflation cooled (CPI ~3.5% annual, softer month-on-month), but rate-cut odds have whipsawed all week — so expect the meeting itself, not the data, to set the tone. No obvious macro landmine before then.

Smart money (from the wiki)

Jordi Visser (12 Jul): the last few weeks were a "speed-crash and leverage flush," not a top — memory shortage now runs "beyond 2030," and he's flagged NVIDIA at a decade-cheap valuation. Today's NVDA flip-to-Leading rhymes with that. InvestAnswers (8 Jul): summer is for sniping dips — NVDA's $190–195 support is holding (price ~$212), AVGO's ~$360 shelf is the reversal line, MU's long-term target ~$1,600, and he'd still avoid Marvell ("broke down") — which today's tape agrees with (MRVL still ~11% below its 50-day).

The fresh divergence

This week's newest wiki call (Limitless, 22 Jul) was "power is the next memory trade" — with GE Vernova as "the TSMC of power." Today power's flagship missed and fell −8.6% while memory (MU) held its ground. The older 19 Jun note already warned GEV was priced for its backlog; today's drop is the first real test of that caveat. The theme isn't broken — the backlog is genuinely huge — but "buy the power trade" is not a one-way bet at these multiples.
