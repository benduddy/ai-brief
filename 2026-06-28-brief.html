Weekend Signal Brief

28 June 2026 · 48 names · benchmark QQQ · data Fri close · synced 20:16 UTC

Friday closed the book on the AI trade's worst week since March: the narrow memory melt-up gave way to a broad cooling. Thirteen names flipped status — five of last week's red-hot leaders came off the boil back to merely Leading (a healthy reset), but a wider band rolled over the other way into Basing or Neutral. Bloom Energy cratered −18%, Vertiv and Qualcomm broke their uptrends, and Nvidia quietly slipped below the support line a source called the floor a week ago. The selloff was about the whole tape de-risking, not any one name breaking.

Market Backdrop

the week the AI trade de-risked
TAPE · Indices & toneNasdaq −4.6% w/w
NeutralSOX −5.3%worst week since Mar '25

What happened

The Nasdaq fell −4.6% on the week and posted a fifth straight down day Friday; the Philadelphia semiconductor index (SOX) dropped −5.3%, its worst week since March 2025. The S&P slid −2% but the Dow rose +0.6% and small-caps (IWM) gained — money rotated out of large-cap AI and into healthcare, real estate and utilities.

Why

Three things stacked up: a report that OpenAI may push its IPO to 2027 (reviving questions about how all this AI capex gets funded), worries about debt-funded data-centre spending, and fear of a more hawkish Fed. Falling GPU rental prices (a B200 hour dropped ~31% in three weeks) added to the "is demand cooling?" narrative.

Smart money (wiki)

Jordi Visser (28 Jun, fresh): calls it rotation, not a top — hyperscalers had a brutal −14% month but breadth (equal-weight S&P near highs, IWM up 3 weeks running) and the 200-day are intact; he says the hawkish-Fed move "was nothing" and has fully unwound. InvestAnswers (24 Jun): reads Micron's blowout as proof "AI is not slowing down," structurally bullish memory. They diverge on whether the memory move is still cheap (IA: yes) or now just hot (Jordi trimmed MU at $600–700).

Read

A textbook digestion week — leaders cooling, laggards catching a defensive bid, no break in the underlying trend yet. The tell to watch is whether the rotation stays within risk (healthcare/utilities) or the 200-day lines start to crack. They haven't.
